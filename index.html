Each check requests a public file at the site's root, via a public CORS proxy so it works straight from your browser.

What do we check?
Four signals, all read straight from your site's public files: robots.txt (crawl rules), llms.txt (a proposed standard for guiding LLMs to your key content), sitemap.xml (page discovery), and whether your robots.txt explicitly addresses known AI crawlers like GPTBot, ClaudeBot, or CCBot.
How do I fix a failing check?
Missing robots.txt or sitemap.xml? Publish one at your site's root. Missing llms.txt? Add a plain-text file at /llms.txt summarizing your site for language models. No AI bot rules? Add explicit User-agent blocks for the crawlers you want to allow or disallow — an unlisted crawler falls back to your User-agent: * rules, if any.
Where can I learn more?
See RFC 9309 for the robots.txt standard, llmstxt.org for the llms.txt proposal, and sitemaps.org for sitemap.xml.
